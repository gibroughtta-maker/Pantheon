Plato, Crito — selected passages
Translation: Benjamin Jowett (1892), Public Domain

— On not returning injustice with injustice —

Then we ought not to retaliate or render evil for evil to any one, whatever
evil we may have suffered from him. Nor ought any one who is injured to
return the injury, for on no account can it be right to do an injustice.

— On the rule of law and the social compact —

Are we to say that we are never intentionally to do wrong, or that in one
way we ought and in another way we ought not to do wrong, or is doing wrong
always evil and dishonourable, as I was just now saying, and as has been
already acknowledged by us?

Crito, my friend, our duty is to obey the laws, even when they decide
against us, so long as we have not first persuaded them otherwise. To
escape now would be to wrong the very laws under whose protection we have
lived our whole lives.

— On the few who matter vs the many —

We must do what the multitude says, and fear what they say? My good friend,
I will tell you what I think. The opinion of the many is to be regarded,
but only of those who have understanding. We should not fear the opinion of
the many, but of the one man who has understanding of justice and injustice.

— On living well, not merely living —

Not life, but a good life, is to be chiefly valued. The good life and the
just life are the same. Therefore, my dear friend, in considering whether
to take this course or some other, the only proper question is whether it
is just or unjust, not whether it is safe or pleasant.

— On reason guided by inquiry —

I am one of those who are willing to be refuted if I say anything which is
not true, and I am willing to refute any one else who says what is not true.
For I think that to be refuted is no less a gain than to refute, in so far
as it is a greater gain to be delivered from the greatest of evils oneself
than to deliver another.

— On the agreement freely made —

He who has experience of the manner in which we order justice and
administer the State, and still remains, has entered into an implied
contract that he will do as we command him.
